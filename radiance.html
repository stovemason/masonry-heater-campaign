The 24-Hour Cycle — Where Design Actually Matters
This is where masonry heater engineering makes the difference. After a single firing, the surface heats up and then slowly cools. Total radiant power (Stefan-Boltzmann Law) is proportional to temperature to the fourth power — so a small drop in temperature means a large drop in output. The chart below shows three heater types over 24 hours. The area under each curve is the total therapeutic radiant energy delivered to you. A massive Grundofen with a large surface area delivers moderate, steady warmth for 20+ hours. A thin-walled system heater peaks high but falls fast — more energy wasted as convection during the hot phase, less useful radiation in the long tail. The comfort zone (green band) is where the surface is warm enough to heat the room but cool enough to touch.

Why Clay and Ceramic Win

At the same temperature, unglazed ceramic (ε 0.90) radiates 18× more infrared than polished steel (ε 0.05). Even compared to a painted radiator (ε 0.84), ceramic surfaces deliver ~7% more radiant energy — and critically, their thermal mass keeps them radiating for hours longer. The combination of high emissivity + slow thermal conductivity + massive heat capacity is unique to traditional masonry heater materials.
