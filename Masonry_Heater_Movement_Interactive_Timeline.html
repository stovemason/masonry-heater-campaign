Detailed Timeline

The Foundations (1980–2000)

Tradition meets regulation ▾
1986
Austrian Kachelofenverband (KOV)
Professional association of tile stove builders established. Centuries of Kachelofen tradition formalised into professional standards.
1988
MHA Founded + EPA NSPS Exemption
Masonry Heater Association of North America founded. EPA NSPS exempts appliances >800 kg from emission testing requirements.
1990
Austrian 15a B-VG Framework
Federal provinces begin harmonising emission requirements for small heating installations under 15a B-VG constitutional articles.
1992
OMNI In-Home Study
OMNI Environmental tests 5 masonry heaters in homes. PM 3.2 g/kg average — 81% reduction vs conventional stoves. Foundation of EPA exemption rationale.

Standards & Science (2001–2012)

EN 15544, Lopez Labs, calculator development ▾
2001
TU Vienna Validates Calculator
Technical University of Vienna combustion lab verifies the KOV's proprietary calculation software against physical test data. Calculation = legal testing.
2004
Lopez Labs Facility Opens
Permanent test facility established at Masonry Stove Builders, Shawville QC. Begins building world's largest masonry heater performance database.
2006
185+ Test Run Database
Lopez Labs / MHA Research Laboratory begins systematic testing programme. 185+ documented runs by 2019. PM averaging 0.97 g/kg normal operation.
2009
EN 15544 Published
European Standard EN 15544 published by CEN — calculation method for site-built masonry heaters. Based on Austrian Kachelofen methodology.
2009
BS EN 15544:2009 Adopted
EN 15544 adopted as British Standard. No regulatory function. No government awareness of its significance.
2010
StoveMason Begins Operations
Masonry Heater Builders (StoveMason) established in Scotland. HETAS registered. Builds to BS EN 15544, member of MHA and Austrian KOV.

Recognition & Growth (2013–2020)

AFPMA founded, EPA negotiations, French tax code ▾
2013
AFPMA Founded
Association Française du Poêle Maçonné Artisanal created. ~40 artisans from ~60 professionals nationally. Explicitly modeled on the American MHA.
2014
EPA Proposes Subpart RRRR
EPA proposes first-ever federal standards for masonry heaters. MHA engages with software calculator alternative to lab testing.
2014
MHA-AFPMA Calculator Collaboration
MHA begins working with Damien Lehmann (AFPMA/France) to adapt EN 15544 calculator for North American heater types.
2015
15a B-VG Art.5(4) — Calc = Test
Austrian federal law explicitly recognises EN 15544 calculation as legally equivalent to physical type testing. 5-pollutant compliance.
2015
EPA Backs Off — Exemption Maintained
EPA does not finalise Subpart RRRR. Exemption maintained. EPA allows MHA time to develop calculator software and test methods.
2016
ADEME Report Published
French environment agency ADEME publishes 'Les poêles de masse artisanaux en France' — first official recognition document.
2017
First Condar + EPA M5G Simultaneous Test
Hearth Lab Solutions, Vermont. First time Condar portable sampler runs simultaneously with EPA Method 5G on a masonry heater.
2018
Research Laboratory Established
AFPMA opens measurement and research laboratory in Montendre (Charente-Maritime), directed by Damien Lehmann. Begins calibrating calculator software.
2019
MHA Comments to EPA ANPR
Detailed comments requesting accommodation for masonry heaters, software certification approach, and model line certification.
2020
EN 15544 in French Tax Code
NF EN 15544 inscribed in Article 18 bis of the French Tax Code. Masonry heaters gain access to CITE tax credits and MaPrimeRénov subsidies.

The New Era (2021–2027)

FIRECALC, certification, UK opportunity ▾
2021
AFPMA Joins VEUKO
AFPMA joins the European federation of tile stove associations (VEUKO, 19 countries). Builds alliance with Austrian KOV.
2022
Ecodesign Overlay
EU Ecodesign Directive 2015/1185 takes effect. Austrian system adds Ecodesign requirements on top of existing 15a B-VG framework.
2022
ADEME: 'Most Performant at Usage'
ADEME officially states the masonry heater is 'le plus performant à l'usage' (most performant in use) of all wood heating appliances.
2023
CQP Professional Certification Created
Certificate of Professional Qualification (CQP) for 'Poêlier — constructeur mainteneur de poêle maçonné artisanal' registered on national RNCP directory.
2023
BS EN 15544:2023 Updated
Updated British Standard published. Still no regulatory recognition, no pathway for site-built heaters in Smoke Control Areas.
2024
First 7 Poêliers Certified
First cohort of 7 masonry heater builders certified via VAE (validation of prior experience). Jury held at Lycée des Métiers du Bâtiment, Felletin.
2025
EPA Consent Decree — NSPS Review
EPA agrees to review and potentially update NSPS for residential wood heaters. Masonry heater scope unclear.
2025
FIRECALC AFPMA Software Presented
New calculation software developed by Guillaume Augais presented at annual colloquium. Extends beyond Austrian heater types. Open-source thermal study tool also released.
2025
First National Training Cohort
9 students begin first formal poêlier training programme in France, partnered with GRETA Limousin.
2026
First National Masonry Heater Day
14 March 2026 — AFPMA coordinates nationwide open-door events. Poêliers across France invite public to experience masonry heaters.
2026
Defra Consultation Opens
Defra smoke control consultation opens. Does not mention masonry heaters, EN 15544, or calculation-based compliance. Closes 19 March 2026.
2026
Evidence Dossier Submitted
Cross-jurisdictional evidence dossier submitted to Defra. 11 regimes compared. Case made for BS EN 15544 recognition.
2027
UK MHA Founded? →
The opportunity: form a UK Masonry Heater Association. Follow the French AFPMA model. Build the same pathway to recognition.

🇬🇧 Where the UK Stands — March 2026

✗No masonry heater association exists
✗EN 15544 adopted as BS EN 15544:2023 but has zero regulatory function
✗No calculation-based compliance pathway for site-built heaters
✗No professional qualification or training programme
✗No government subsidies for masonry heater installation
✗Defra 2026 consultation does not mention masonry heaters
✗A handful of builders operate without formal recognition
✓One HETAS-registered masonry heater builder exists (StoveMason, Scotland) — building to BS EN 15544, member of both MHA and Austrian KOV
✓BS EN 15544:2023 is already a British Standard — the legal foundation exists
✓HETAS infrastructure exists — could certify masonry heater builders like it certifies stove installers
The French precedent is the proof. France had no masonry heater culture in 2013. Thirteen years later, AFPMA has achieved government recognition, tax code inclusion, professional certification, subsidies, its own research laboratory, custom calculation software, and ~1,000 installations per year. The UK has all the same building blocks — BS EN 15544, a HETAS framework, individual skilled builders — but lacks the association to connect them. Everything France achieved started with one thing: artisans organising.
